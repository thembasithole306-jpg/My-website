Poem 3

The Day Your Eyes Forgot Me

Hey!

I don’t even know if you’ll respond to this,
Or even bother to read it, at least.
Because lately our eyes hold different worlds, different iris,
And somewhere along the way, one of us became a beast.

A beast that swings in the dark, blind,
Strong enough to destroy without a clue,
Shattering everything that once felt kind,
Like it was never loved, like none of it was true.

They say strength is never crying,
But silence turns the heart into a grave,
Holding it in feels like slowly dying,
And every lie becomes a wave.

I remember how we laughed together,
Under warm skies and perfect weather,
I felt lighter than any feather,
Dreaming a future we built together.

And that joy felt real to me,
Nothing about it seemed snappy,
My whole body reacted in a daze,
Not knowing why my chest felt so happy.

My friends swore the signs meant something,
And I was innocent enough to believe,
Until you stopped the one thing that kept us running, loving,
Leaving me sliced and salted like meat you cleave.

I questioned myself, "What did I do?",
For you to shift so fast, become so cold,
You changed out of nowhere, out of the blue,
And masked your heart behind a lie you sold.

So I dug, followed every clue,
Because answers matter when pain knocks louder,
Found out a spare wheel only matters with a puncture too,
But honestly, I had sensed the disaster.

Now I know how it feels to be loved by lips, not actions,
While someone else takes center stage,
I was just a filler in your life's distractions,
You called me air, but he became the whole sky’s page.

You thought your secrets were sealed tight,
Safe inside darkness like a hidden cave,
Until I said, “Don’t think I was born last night,
I know about you and John, the love you gave.”

You feared I’d hurt you, feared a swing,
But I had no intention to harm or fight,
’Cause when you cheated, you had a ring,
Your own choice, your own light.

No wonder we stopped seeing eye to eye,
No wonder your voice could not say hi,
Your eyes forgot me long before,
That’s why I finally showed you the door.

I never wanted revenge or score,
I simply chose myself over war,
I know my worth,
It’s been in me since birth.

My mother raised a gentleman,
A man who handles fire like a fireman,
I don’t know what you saw in that other guy,
But one thing’s certain, your loss, not mine.
