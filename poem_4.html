Poem 4 – A Note to Self

Home | All Poems

Hey!

This poem won’t be going to you.
I guess it’s like a little note to self.
It’s time I become true,
Take the books off the shelf.

It’s been long; they are even dusty.
I was lost in the act of “love.”
Even metal decays and gets rusty;
Nothing stays forever, except the one above.

I wished our bond could be forever,
Because being with you was always better.
Yet life has its ways, however
No one knows the future, no man is that clever.

We shared love so true;
Every day all I thought about was you.
Time we spent together—quality,
I never knew one of us lacked honesty.

You stayed with me for what I offered,
Not for the heart or love.
Then, in a blink, you left like water,
Evaporating in the sun, rising like a dove.

You tried to pin all this on me;
When they asked, you said, “ask him.”
Chasing the green, all about the tree,
I never thought I’d be left in the dim.

Your friends believed I was to blame,
All under your control like pawns in a game.
Now they hold hate,
But for me it carried no weight.

You turned them all against,
Well, that’s what you thought.
Overthinking every move, all this was sensed
We reached a tie—a knot.

I had to cut all ties,
Because I told you countless times I hate lies.
By all ties, I meant everything connected to you,
So I could stay with only what’s true.

Clearly, you were the rotten potato,
And it affects the whole bag.
You were even happy to see me low,
Your head full of air, like an airbag.

You thought you pulled me down,
But in reality, you were the clown.
You hated me for not giving my card,
Damn, girl, your life must have been hard.

You threw dirt, trying to kill me,
But you grew a wildflower instead.
Setting me free,
Like water running after a long day—a shower.

You thought you were the prize,
But you were just a salty spice.
Now that you are gone, life feels nice,
Like a soft drink on a hot day with ice.

Since you left,
I feel something lifted from my back—a knife.
I survived the wildlife,
Turned shelf-life into real life.

A piece of advice to myself and all who read:
Know your worth, don’t stay where you’ll end up broken.
Love may hurt, but it was never meant to kill.
Love is not perfect, yet it conquers all when hearts are true.
When they don’t love you, leave, because you deserve more.
