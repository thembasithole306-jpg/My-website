INK TO SOUL

A place where words speak and hearts listen

Mirrors

Hey!

Can I ask you a question?

When was the last time
you looked into a mirror
and saw yourself?

Not your face.

Not your hair.

Not the smile you've practiced so well.

You.

The person hiding behind all of it.

The person who cries in silence.

The person carrying battles nobody knows about.

The person who keeps saying,
"I'll be okay,"
while slowly falling apart.

Because mirrors are strange.

They show us everything
except the things that hurt the most.

They show the eyes,
but not the tears.

The smile,
but not the pain.

The body,
but not the burden.

The reflection,
but not the story.

And maybe that's why people fear them.

Not because of what they see.

But because of what they don't.

I have stood before mirrors
after being betrayed.

After being abandoned.

After giving my all
and receiving almost nothing back.

And do you know what scared me?

It wasn't the reflection.

It was realizing that
the person staring back at me
looked tired.

Not physically.

Soul tired.

The kind of tired
that sleep cannot fix.

The kind that comes from
being strong for too long.

The kind that comes from
loving people who only loved
what they could take from you.

The kind that comes from
bleeding in places
nobody can see.

The mirror saw it all.

The nights I questioned myself.

The days I hated myself.

The moments I smiled
while my heart was collapsing.

The times I apologized
just to keep people from leaving.

The times I broke myself
trying to keep others whole.

And still...

The mirror never judged me.

It never laughed.

It never walked away.

It simply stood there.

Waiting.

Waiting for the day
I would stop looking at my wounds
and start looking at my survival.

Because maybe that's the purpose of a mirror.

Not to show us what is wrong.

But to remind us
of everything that failed to destroy us.

So if you look into a mirror today
and all you can see are your flaws,

look again.

Look deeper.

Past the scars.

Past the failures.

Past the mistakes.

Because the person standing there
has survived every heartbreak,
every disappointment,
every dark night,
every battle they thought would end them.

And somehow...

They're still standing.

And if that isn't strength,

what is?
