Poem 1

Fragments of a Heart

Home | All Poems

Hey!

I’m writing this not because I want to,
But because my chest is heavy
And every time I breathe,
Something inside me turns and twists
Like a knife being pushed slowly.

You said forever—
And I believed you.
Not because you were perfect,
But because your voice felt warm
And your eyes looked honest.

I guess I should have known that
Humans lie even when they don’t mean to.
Love fades even when it feels strong.
And hearts break quietly
Long before the final goodbye.

I tried, I swear I did.
I held every piece together
Even when they were cutting me.
But you let go first,
And everything fell apart after.

Now I’m here, writing this,
Not to make you feel bad,
But to free myself
From the weight of pretending
That I’m still whole.
