Poem 1

Hey!

It's me again
The reason I am writing this is because I have no one else but myself to blame
Since you left, my chest feels heavy, my heart wrapped in chains
To remove this weight, I feel I’ll need a crane.

When I planned the future, you were always in the frame
I know it sounds insane, but your image lives in my brain
I even gave you a nickname, thinking it would forever remain
It felt like I was high on something I couldn’t explain
A rush I couldn’t restrain, a storm I couldn’t contain
And it’s funny how you forgot my name, as if I never existed.

And when you said you can’t
My mind erupted like Octane, ten thoughts racing, none clear
Each choice like lightning striking, each one I fear
After realizing the decision you took, a sharp pulse hit my head
A migraine reminding me the future I saw for us had fled
Dreams of us reaching Mars now float in empty space
And I’m left tracing the ghost of your face.

I renamed our memory a losing game
Because I wandered in someone else’s domain
How can I call it a win when there’s nothing to gain?
How can I move forward when the past still remains?

I remember the nights I whispered your name
My pillow soaked with unshed tears, my soul aflame
I dreamed of laughter, of sunlit mornings in your eyes
Now every silent room echoes your goodbye.

And yet, I cling to the fragments, the small sparks of “us”
Though they burn in my chest, though the fire turns to dust
Perhaps one day, these chains will fall
And I’ll find a way to rise, to stand tall.

But tonight, I write
Because the pain is too loud to keep inside
And even if no one reads these words
They carry my heart, raw, unbroken, wide.
